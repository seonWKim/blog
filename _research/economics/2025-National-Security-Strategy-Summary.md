# Summary: 2025 National Security Strategy of the United States

**Document Date:** November 2025
**Author:** President Donald J. Trump Administration
**Source:** 2025-National-Security-Strategy.pdf

---

## Overview

This National Security Strategy, issued by President Trump in November 2025, represents a fundamental shift from
post-Cold War American foreign policy toward an "America First" doctrine. The strategy claims the administration has
achieved peace in 8 conflicts within 9 months and seeks to rebalance America's global commitments.

---

## **Key Strategic Priorities**

### 1. **Border Security & End of Mass Migration**

- **Border security declared the primary element of national security**
- Full control over borders, immigration, and transportation networks
- Drug cartels and gangs designated as Foreign Terrorist Organizations
- Use of military force, including lethal force, to defeat cartels
- Mass migration era declared over
- Protection from terrorism, drugs, espionage, and human trafficking

### 2. **Economic Security as National Security**

#### **Trade & Commerce:**

- Rebalancing trade relationships using tariffs and reciprocal agreements
- Ending predatory practices, IP theft, and unfair subsidies
- Reducing trade deficits (especially with China)
- Goal: Grow US economy from $30 trillion (2025) to $40 trillion (2030s)
- Opposition to barriers to US exports and anti-competitive practices

#### **Reindustrialization:**

- Reshoring manufacturing and production
- Rebuilding defense industrial base
- Creating widespread prosperity for American workers
- Industrial strength as "highest priority of national economic policy"
- Using tariffs and new technologies to favor domestic production
- Ensuring never reliant on adversaries for critical products

#### **Energy Dominance:**

- Unleashing oil, gas, coal, and nuclear production
- Rejecting "climate change" and "Net Zero" ideologies
- Making US a leading energy exporter
- Energy as strategic tool for alliances and power projection
- Cheap, abundant energy to fuel growth and AI development

#### **Supply Chain Security:**

- Never dependent on adversaries for critical goods
- Securing access to critical minerals and rare earth elements
- Expanding American control over strategic resources
- Intelligence Community monitoring of supply chain vulnerabilities
- Re-securing independent access to defense-critical materials

#### **Financial Sector Dominance:**

- Maintaining dollar as global reserve currency
- Preserving leadership in capital markets and digital finance
- Using financial leverage as strategic tool
- Leveraging dynamic free market system for global advantage

#### **Defense Industrial Base Revival:**

- National mobilization to innovate powerful defenses at low cost
- Produce capable, modern systems and munitions at scale
- Re-shore defense industrial supply chains
- Address drone/missile cost asymmetry
- Full range of capabilities from low-cost to high-end systems

---

## **Military & Defense Strategy**

### **Core Military Priorities:**

- **$1 trillion military investment**
- World's most powerful, lethal, technologically advanced military
- Nuclear deterrent + "Golden Dome" missile defense for homeland
- Removal of "radical gender ideology" and DEI from Armed Forces
- Every servicemember proud of country and confident in mission
- Focus on: AI, quantum computing, autonomous systems, undersea, space, nuclear domains
- Win wars quickly and decisively with lowest casualties
- Next-generation missile defenses

### **Burden Sharing Revolution:**

- **NATO allies must spend 5% of GDP on defense** (Hague Commitment - up from 2%)
- Allies must assume primary responsibility for their regions
- End of US as "Atlas" holding up world order
- Shift from global US presence to strategic partnerships
- Targeted partnerships using economic tools to align incentives
- Countries that take more regional responsibility get favorable treatment
- Technology sharing and defense procurement benefits for aligned partners

## **Regional Strategies**

### **Western Hemisphere** (HIGHEST PRIORITY)

#### **"Trump Corollary to the Monroe Doctrine":**

- Reasserting US preeminence in the Hemisphere
- Denying non-Hemispheric competitors access to vital assets
- Military readjustment: deploying forces FROM other regions TO Hemisphere
- No foreign forces or threatening capabilities in Hemisphere
- No foreign ownership/control of strategically vital assets

#### **"Enlist and Expand" Strategy:**

**Enlist:**

- Control migration and neutralize cartels
- Near-shore manufacturing partnerships
- Develop local private economies
- Regional champions to create stability beyond their borders

**Expand:**

- Cultivating new partners
- Becoming Hemisphere's partner of choice
- Joint development of strategic resources

#### **Key Actions:**

- Coast Guard/Navy presence for sea lane control
- Targeted military deployments to secure border and defeat cartels
- Use of lethal force where necessary
- Establishing/expanding access in strategic locations
- Commercial diplomacy using tariffs and reciprocal trade
- Counter Chinese and foreign influence in Latin America
- National Security Council identifying strategic points/resources in Hemisphere
- Rolling back adversarial outside influence (ports, infrastructure, assets)
- US Government helping American companies compete for contracts
- Financing programs to support US goods purchases abroad

### **Asia/Indo-Pacific** - Win Economic Future, Prevent Military Confrontation

#### **Economic Competition:**

**Current Situation:**

- China's exports to US fell from 4% to 2% of China's GDP due to tariffs
- China now exports through proxy countries
- China's exports to low-income countries nearly 4x exports to US
- Indo-Pacific is ~50% of world GDP (PPP), 1/3 nominal GDP

**Strategy:**

- Working with allies (US $30T + allies $35T = $65T combined economy)
- Counter predatory practices together
- Focus on low- and middle-income countries where growth will occur
- Securing semiconductor supply chains (Taiwan critical)
- Rebalancing economic relationship with China - reciprocity and fairness
- Trade should be balanced, focused on non-sensitive factors

**US Actions:**

- Protect economy from predatory subsidies, unfair trading, IP theft
- End job destruction and deindustrialization
- Secure supply chains and critical materials access
- Stop fentanyl precursor exports
- Counter propaganda and cultural subversion
- Invest in cutting-edge military/dual-use technology (AI, quantum, autonomous systems)
- Align allies' export controls with US
- Present partners with inducements (tech cooperation, defense purchases, capital market access)

#### **Military Deterrence:**

**Taiwan & First Island Chain:**

- **Taiwan defense is priority** - maintaining "no unilateral change" policy
- Taiwan critical for semiconductors and splitting NE/SE Asia theaters
- Building military capable of denying aggression in First Island Chain
- 1/3 of global shipping passes through South China Sea
- Must keep sea lanes open, free of "tolls," not subject to arbitrary closure

**Allied Responsibilities:**

- Japan, South Korea, Taiwan, Australia must increase defense spending
- Allow greater US military access to ports and facilities
- Invest in capabilities to deter aggression
- Interlink maritime security along First Island Chain
- Create balance unfavorable to aggressors

**US Military Posture:**

- Harden and strengthen Western Pacific presence
- Focus on domains where US has advantages (undersea, space, nuclear)
- Improve network defense and offensive cyber capabilities
- Maintain surveillance of threats to critical infrastructure

## **Key Principles & Doctrines**

### **Core Strategic Principles:**

1. **Focused Definition of National Interest** - Not everything is US concern; focus on core interests
2. **Peace Through Strength** - Strength deters conflict and enables peacemaking
3. **Predisposition to Non-Interventionism** - High bar for intervention; respect for separate and equal nations
4. **Flexible Realism** - Accept differing governing systems; don't impose democracy
5. **Primacy of Nations** - Nation-state is fundamental unit; oppose transnationalism
6. **Sovereignty and Respect** - Protect US sovereignty from international organizations, foreign influence
7. **Balance of Power** - Prevent global or regional domination by adversaries
8. **Pro-American Worker** - Not just pro-growth; prioritize US workers over "global talent"
9. **Fairness** - End free-riding, demand reciprocity from allies in defense and trade
10. **Competence and Merit** - Eliminate DEI; promote based on ability

### **Strategic Approach:**

- **Pragmatic without being "pragmatist"**
- **Realistic without being "realist"**
- **Principled without being "idealistic"**
- **Muscular without being "hawkish"**
- **Restrained without being "dovish"**
- **Motivated by what works for America - "America First"**

## **Potential Risks & Concerns**

### **Economic Risks:**

1. **Trade Wars** - Aggressive tariff use could disrupt global commerce
2. **Inflation** - Tariffs may increase consumer prices
3. **Supply Chain Disruption** - Decoupling from China could cause short-term pain
4. **Ally Retaliation** - Trading partners may impose counter-tariffs
5. **Dollar Dominance Threat** - Aggressive use of financial leverage may push countries away from dollar

### **Military & Security Risks:**

1. **Alliance Strains** - Demanding 5% GDP defense spending may strain relationships
2. **Credibility Questions** - Burden-sharing emphasis raises doubts about US commitments (especially Taiwan)
3. **Power Vacuums** - Withdrawal from "peripheral" regions may create opportunities for adversaries
4. **Ukraine Settlement** - Rapid war termination may not favor Ukrainian interests or European security
5. **China Miscalculation** - Deterrence strategy could fail, leading to conflict over Taiwan

### **Diplomatic Risks:**

1. **Isolation** - Rejection of climate policy and international institutions could isolate US
2. **Soft Power Loss** - Reduced engagement may diminish American influence
3. **Democratic Backsliding** - Support for "patriotic parties" in Europe might embolden authoritarians
4. **Middle East Instability** - Reduced presence could allow conflicts to reignite
5. **Russia Emboldened** - Quick Ukraine settlement might encourage Russian aggression elsewhere

### **Domestic Risks:**

1. **Labor Shortages** - Anti-immigration stance may reduce workforce
2. **Competitiveness Loss** - Restrictions on "global talent" could hurt innovation
3. **Social Division** - Emphasis on "traditional families" and rejection of DEI may increase polarization
4. **Economic Transition Pain** - Reindustrialization takes time; workers may suffer during transition

## **Bottom Line Assessment**

### **Fundamental Reorientation:**

This strategy represents a complete pivot in US foreign policy around:

1. **Economic nationalism** over globalization
2. **Hemispheric focus** over global presence
3. **Burden-sharing** over US primacy
4. **Deterrence** over intervention
5. **National sovereignty** over international institutions
6. **Traditional values** over progressive ideology
7. **Core interests** over universal engagement
8. **Bilateral deals** over multilateral frameworks
9. **Energy dominance** over climate action
10. **Merit** over diversity/equity/inclusion

### **Key Characteristics:**

**Strengths:**

- Clear prioritization of interests
- Realistic assessment of limited resources
- Focus on economic competitiveness
- Emphasis on allied burden-sharing
- Protection of American workers
- Strong deterrence posture

**Weaknesses:**

- May underestimate interdependence
- Risk of alliance deterioration
- Could create power vacuums
- Potential for trade war escalation
- May reduce American soft power
- Climate change impact ignored

### **Historical Context:**

This strategy explicitly rejects:

- Post-Cold War consensus
- Liberal internationalism
- Democracy promotion
- Nation-building
- Free trade orthodoxy
- Multilateral institutions
- Climate change as priority

Instead, it embraces:

- Jacksonian nationalism
- Hamiltonian industrial policy
- Monroe Doctrine revival (Western Hemisphere focus)
- Peace through strength (Reagan-era concept)
- Transactional alliances
- Economic nationalism
- Cultural traditionalism

### **Long-term Implications:**

**If Successful:**

- US industrial base rebuilt
- Middle class strengthened
- Allies more capable and self-reliant
- Reduced risk of major wars
- American economic preeminence maintained
- Borders secured
- Cultural renewal

**If Unsuccessful:**

- Allies may pursue independent paths (including nuclear weapons)
- China could dominate Asia economically and militarily
- US could be isolated economically and diplomatically
- Trade wars could trigger recession
- Global instability from reduced US engagement
- Climate impacts could overwhelm economic gains

### **Core Philosophy:**

The strategy can be summarized as: **"America First" nationalism combined with pragmatic realism, seeking prosperity at
home through economic protection and reindustrialization, peace abroad through strength and deterrence, and security
through border control and hemispheric dominance, while demanding that allies shoulder their own defense burdens and
trading partners engage fairly and reciprocally.**
